## Jack Dorsey’s Block Launches Buzz: An AI-Native Slack Competitor

### The decentralized and model-agnostic workspace puts human teams and autonomous AI agents in the same group chats.

In a direct challenge to established enterprise platforms, Twitter and Block co-founder Jack Dorsey has officially announced the launch of Buzz, an open-source and decentralized workplace chat client. Built by Dorsey’s company Block, the app integrates human collaboration, code management, and autonomous AI agents into a single unified window. As organizations shift toward agentic workflows, Buzz represents a radical attempt to decouple daily business communication from traditional proprietary silos.

## Key Details

Announced on Tuesday, July 21, 2026, Buzz is designed as a direct competitor to both Slack and GitHub. Built under the auspices of Dorsey's company Block—which also operates Square, Cash App, Afterpay, and Tidal—the product is built from the ground up to support modern, AI-integrated startups. The app's desktop client is already available for download on macOS, Windows, and Linux, and its complete codebase has been open-sourced on GitHub.

Key features of the platform include:
- **Model-Agnostic Engine**: Buzz does not tie teams to a specific AI vendor. Users can run OpenAI’s GPT models, Anthropic's Claude, Google's Gemini, or self-hosted open-weights models in the same environment.
- **Decentralized and Self-Sovereign**: Organizations can run their own independent Buzz instances to maintain absolute control over their operational data.
- **Unified Workspace**: It merges Slack-like messaging with native code-repository integrations, allowing team members and AI agents to execute commits and review PRs directly from chat windows.

### What This Means

The launch of Buzz marks a critical milestone in the transition from simple chatbots to fully autonomous agentic workflows. In traditional corporate setups, AI tools are typically relegated to sidebars or treated as isolated APIs. Buzz, on the other hand, elevates AI agents to first-class citizens. By placing human employees and digital agents in the same channel, Block is normalizing the concept of 'hybrid intelligence' teams.

This setup allows agents to receive commands naturally in plain English, collaborate on code, and output results where everyone can see them, vastly improving transparency. It also gives rise to a more competitive market where companies are no longer locked into expensive, centralized cloud services for simple team chat functions.

### Technical Breakdown

To support its decentralized and model-agnostic philosophy, Buzz utilizes a highly modular and secure technical architecture:

- **Local and Remote Hosting**: Teams can self-host their Buzz instance, completely bypassing the security issues and subscription fees associated with centralized SaaS models.
- **Open-Source Customizability**: Because the entire codebase is open-source, developers can build bespoke modules, change UX elements, or integrate highly specialized, fine-tuned corporate models.
- **Universal Agent Protocol**: Buzz utilizes standardized APIs to communicate with agents, meaning standard software engineering bots can be integrated with minimal configuration.
- **Integrated GitHub Management**: The app natively connects with version control systems, enabling human developers and AI coding agents to collaborate on repositories without switching context.

## Industry Impact

For the broader enterprise software market, Buzz represents a shot across the bow of industry giants like Salesforce-owned Slack and Microsoft-owned Teams. Traditional collaboration suites are struggling to retroactively adapt their databases and security layers to handle agent-to-agent transactions and autonomous bot logins. Block’s solution side-steps these legacy issues by building with AI-agent collaboration as a core design principle.

Additionally, the decentralized architecture addresses a major enterprise pain point: the 'agent security gap' where corporate credentials and proprietary source code are frequently leaked to third-party model providers. By hosting the chat client locally, companies can run open-source LLMs locally, ensuring that no sensitive workspace conversations or data ever leave their private network.

## Looking Ahead

While Buzz is currently in its early stages of development, the backing of a major financial and technology conglomerate like Block ensures it will receive significant resources and development support. As the platform matures, expect Block to integrate its own fintech capabilities, potentially paving the way for autonomous AI agents to initiate and execute financial transactions using Block’s payment infrastructure.

For now, early-stage startups and open-source enthusiasts have a powerful new playground to explore the next generation of collaborative workspace technology.
