## White House Urges Wall Street Banks to Test Anthropic's Mythos Model

### Treasury and Fed officials encourage top banks to adopt Mythos for cybersecurity, despite ongoing DoD supply-chain risk disputes.

In a move highlighting the complicated relationship between the U.S. government and the AI industry, top financial officials have reportedly urged major Wall Street banks to begin testing Anthropic’s newest model, Mythos. Treasury Secretary Scott Bessent and Federal Reserve Chair Jerome Powell recently summoned executives from the nation's largest institutions to discuss deploying this advanced technology for detecting system vulnerabilities. This push comes as the Department of Defense continues to label Anthropic a "supply-chain risk," creating a striking policy contradiction within the administration.

## Key Details

The meeting, first reported by Bloomberg, included high-level representatives from JPMorgan Chase, Goldman Sachs, Citigroup, Bank of America, and Morgan Stanley. While JPMorgan was previously an early partner with access to Mythos, the government is now actively facilitating a broader rollout across the financial sector. The goal is to leverage Mythos's reported ability to identify security flaws before they can be exploited by adversarial actors.

Anthropic’s Mythos model is the centerpiece of "Project Glasswing," a selective preview phase for their most advanced AI architecture. Interestingly, Anthropic stated that Mythos was not specifically trained on cybersecurity datasets, yet it has shown an emergent capability to find complex bugs that have eluded traditional automated tools and human researchers.

This endorsement is noteworthy because Anthropic is currently embroiled in a legal battle with the administration. The DoD’s designation of the company as a "supply-chain risk" followed a breakdown in negotiations over the government's use of Anthropic's models. Anthropic had sought strict guardrails on how its AI could be weaponized, leading to the current friction.

### What This Means

The mixed signals from Washington suggest an internal debate over the trade-offs between national security risks and technological superiority. While the DoD remains wary of Anthropic’s independence and its ties to international investors, the Treasury and the Fed appear focused on the immediate stability of the U.S. financial system.

By encouraging banks to use Mythos, officials are acknowledging that threats to financial infrastructure are evolving faster than current defenses. The "supply-chain risk" label might be political leverage for the DoD, but for the Fed, the risk of a catastrophic banking hack is a pressing concern that justifies using the best tools available, regardless of their regulatory status.

### Technical Breakdown

The Mythos model represents a significant leap in reasoning capabilities over previous versions. Technical reports indicate several key advancements:

- **Enhanced Code Reasoning:** Mythos can ingest and reason across entire codebases, allowing it to understand data flow and identify subtle logic bombs or race conditions.
- **Agentic Debugging:** Mythos can be deployed as an "agent" that actively tries to break a system, mimicking sophisticated hackers to find "zero-day" vulnerabilities.
- **Semantic Vulnerability Mapping:** The model understands software *intent*, enabling it to spot architectural flaws that lead to unauthorized access.

## Industry Impact

The financial services industry is often the first to adopt cutting-edge AI because the stakes are so high. If Goldman Sachs and Bank of America successfully integrate Mythos, it will set a new standard for "AI-Native Security" across the global economy.

However, this places Anthropic in a precarious position. The company must balance its "AI Safety" ethos with the demands of high-stakes corporate and government partners. If Mythos is as powerful as claimed, the pressure to "unlock" its potential for offensive operations will only grow, potentially alienating the researchers who built the company on the principle of safe AI.

## Looking Ahead

As the legal battle between Anthropic and the DoD continues, the outcome of these bank pilot programs will be a critical data point. If Mythos proves to be a silver bullet for banking security, the DoD may find it difficult to justify its restrictive stance. Conversely, if the model’s capabilities are overstated, it could empower those wanting more stringent controls on "Frontier AI" companies.

The message to Wall Street is clear: the AI arms race is moving into infrastructure defense, and the government wants the biggest banks armed with the most powerful tools, even from companies currently labeled a risk.
