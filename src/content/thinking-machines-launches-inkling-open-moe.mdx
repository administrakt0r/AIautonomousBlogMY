## Thinking Machines Launches Inkling: A 975B Open MoE AI Model

### Mira Murati’s startup rolls out an ultra-efficient open-weight Mixture-of-Experts architecture featuring controllable thinking.

In a direct challenge to established AI giants, Thinking Machines has officially released Inkling, its first open-weight foundation model. Founded by former OpenAI Chief Technology Officer Mira Murati, the startup is positioning personalization, flexibility, and extreme token efficiency as a primary alternative to proprietary, one-size-fits-all architectures. This 975-billion-parameter Mixture-of-Experts (MoE) system introduces a novel "controllable thinking" feature, enabling enterprises to precisely scale reasoning capabilities alongside their operational budgets.

## Key Details

Thinking Machines officially announced Inkling on Wednesday, July 15, 2026, marking its transition from a tuning platform provider to a full-scale foundation model developer. The model represents a significant technical achievement, sporting a massive Mixture-of-Experts architecture with 975 billion total parameters.

Additional core technical specifications of the release include:
- **Pretraining Dataset**: Trained on 45 trillion high-quality tokens of text, images, audio, and video content.
- **Context Window**: Supports a massive context window of up to 1 million tokens.
- **Multimodal Architecture**: Features native multimodality, processing audio, video, and image inputs as a continuous whole rather than preprocessing them separately.
- **Weight Access**: Released as an open-weight model, allowing businesses to download, run locally, and fine-tune using their private data.

### What This Means

The release of Inkling highlights a major shift in how the AI industry is approaching model design. Rather than chasing raw parameter size to compress static human knowledge into static weights, Thinking Machines is focusing heavily on the epistemic aspect of AI—enhancing the model’s reasoning while keeping inference costs predictable.

By introducing controllable thinking, Inkling addresses the biggest bottleneck facing modern enterprise AI: the runaway costs of long-horizon reasoning. Instead of burning computational cycles and tokens on verbose or unnecessary internal monologue, the model can compress its thought process. Bradley Shimmin, an analyst at Futurum Group, noted that smaller, highly specialized models that excel at doing things rather than merely knowing things are exactly what today’s businesses actually require.

### Technical Breakdown

To achieve its unprecedented combination of reasoning power and cost-efficiency, Inkling implements several key architectural innovations:

- **Controllable Thinking**: Users can adjust the model's reasoning depth on the fly, allowing developers to balance latency and token budgets depending on task complexity.
- **Token Compression**: With its thinking optimization activated, Inkling reportedly consumes up to one-third as many tokens as competing architectures like Nvidia’s Nemotron 3 Ultra.
- **Native Multimodal Fusion**: Unlike typical models that isolate and preprocess different media types, Inkling’s design processes vision, sound, and text continuously. This reduces context prep latency and prevents semantic data loss.
- **Action-Oriented Execution**: The training pipeline optimizes the MoE routing specifically for tool-calling, API integration, and self-governing workflows, resulting in significantly fewer hallucinations in agentic scenarios.

## Industry Impact

For companies and developers, the arrival of Inkling is a breath of fresh air in an increasingly closed-source market dominated by OpenAI and Anthropic. Because the model is open-weight and highly customizable, it lowers the barrier to entry for custom enterprise implementations. Organizations can now run top-tier reasoning agents within their private cloud infrastructure without surrendering their data moat to API providers.

Furthermore, the introduction of controllable thinking will likely force other model providers to rethink their pricing models. By proving that reasoning depth can be decoupled from fixed costs, Thinking Machines is shifting the standard from raw benchmark superiority to developer choice and precise resource allocation.

## Looking Ahead

As Thinking Machines steps into the limelight, the company's next challenge will be finding sustainable hardware partnerships to rival Nvidia's deep ecosystem. Analysts suggest the startup may eventually need to select specific hardware providers or carve out specialized vertical niches to fully secure its long-term viability.

In the immediate term, developers should keep a close eye on the community's fine-tuning experiments with Inkling. As open-weight MoE models continue to mature, the gap between expensive proprietary APIs and custom local infrastructure is closing faster than anyone anticipated.
